    <h1>Internship Reflections</h1>

    <p>
        This section documents my internship as a Cybersecurity Technician Intern at
        Twigs Naturals, completed as part of CYSE 368. Across approximately 150 internship hours,
        I contributed to governance, risk, and compliance initiatives while
        supporting security documentation, access control, system administration,
        and policy development.
    </p>

    <p>
        The reflections below illustrate how my responsibilities evolved throughout
        the internship, demonstrating increasing independence, technical depth, and
        professional growth.
    </p>
</section>

<section class="section">
    <h2>Internship Timeline</h2>

    <div class="resume-entry">
        <h3>Reflection 1 – Orientation &amp; Governance</h3>
        <p class="resume-meta">First 50 Hours</p>

        <p>
            This reflection captures the beginning of my cybersecurity internship with
            Twigs Naturals, where I was introduced to governance, risk, and compliance
            practices in a real-world environment. During this stage, I contributed to
            a system design document for a cloud-based solution, managed user access
            and policy compliance within the eFense vCISO Portal, and coordinated the
            intake of organizational policies and SOPs.
        </p>

        <p>
            This phase helped me understand how cybersecurity extends beyond technical
            systems to include documentation, access control, policy management, and
            cross-functional collaboration.
        </p>

        <a class="button" href="{{ '/assets/documents/writing-research/internship-reflections/CYSE368_ReflectionPaper1_JamesDavis.pdf' | relative_url }}">View Reflection</a>

    <div class="resume-entry">
        <h3>Reflection 2 – Access Control &amp; Security Improvements</h3>
        <p class="resume-meta">Second 50 Hours</p>

        <p>
            This reflection documents how my responsibilities expanded from monitoring
            and documentation into more active security planning and operational
            improvements. During this phase, I developed an Access Control Matrix and
            application inventory to support role-based access and onboarding
            processes.
        </p>

        <p>
            I also implemented a solution to mitigate spam account creation on the
            company’s WordPress site through a structured method of procedure. This
            work strengthened my understanding of access control, risk reduction,
            secure configuration, and process documentation.
        </p>

        <a class="button" href="{{ '/assets/documents/writing-research/internship-reflections/CYSE368_ReflectionPaper2_JamesDavis.pdf' | relative_url }}">View Reflection</a>
    </div>

    <div class="resume-entry">
        <h3>Reflection 3 – Security Planning &amp; Analysis</h3>
        <p class="resume-meta">Final 50 Hours</p>

        <p>
            This reflection captures the final stage of my internship, where I
            transitioned into more independent analysis and security planning. During
            this stage, I developed an email security enhancement plan involving SPF,
            DKIM, and DMARC, contributed to documenting and validating access control
            models for AppSheet applications, and supported the creation of a data
            archival and integrity framework.
        </p>

        <p>
            This phase strengthened my ability to identify security gaps, design
            structured solutions, and evaluate how policies and controls function in
            practice.
        </p>

        <a class="button" href="{{ '/assets/documents/writing-research/internship-reflections/CYSE368_ReflectionPaper3_JamesDavis.pdf' | relative_url }}">View Reflection</a>
    </div>
